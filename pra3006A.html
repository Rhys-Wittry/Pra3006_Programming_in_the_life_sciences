Migration Viral Infections

The visualizer is an interactive tool designed to showcase the relationships between viruses and their host species, such as Humans, Dogs, Cats, and Rats. It analyzes the similarity of protein sequences across different viruses, providing insights into how closely related these viruses are.
Each node in the graph represents a species, a virus, or a protein, while the edges (links) between nodes illustrate the degree of similarity between protein sequences. The colors of nodes and edges reflect similarity levels, with higher similarities represented by warmer colors. This tool leverages SPARQL queries to retrieve real-time data from the UniProt database, a leading repository of protein sequence and functional information. By using this data, the visualizer ensures accurate and comprehensive insights into viral proteins and their interactions.
The visualizer is particularly useful for exploring potential zoonotic risks, identifying viruses that may share binding sites, or studying the evolutionary relationships between viruses. Users can interact with the graph by hovering over nodes to see details, dragging elements to reorganize the view, or zooming in and out to focus on specific areas.
